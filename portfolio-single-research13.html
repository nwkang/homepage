CAE by Deep Learning

    Asian Congress of Structural and Multidisciplinary Optimization, 2020.
Can Deep Learning Predict the Performance of 3D Structural Designs Based on 2D Structural Designs?

The development of AI algorithms and computing power has raised expectations for generative design technologies that create new design options without human intervention. Generation design can automatically create thousands of topology design options by varying the problem definition parameters of topology optimization. In our previous work, Oh et al. (2019) proposed a deep generative design framework that iteratively integrates generative models of deep learning and generative designs. This framework uses only a small amount of previous design data to create vast amounts of new design options that meet both engineering performance and product aesthetics. Previous work focused on creating 2D structural designs, but it is necessary to evaluate the performance of 3D structures to ensure the safety of the final product. However, even with the latest technology, creating thousands of 3D structures directly is very difficult. Therefore, the research question is how to predict the performance of 3D structures based on 2D structural designs.
The current study proposes a design evaluation process using deep learning models which consists of five stages: (1) dimensionality reduction, (2) sampling in latent space, (3) converting 2D designs to 3D designs, (4) computer-aided engineering (CAE) simulation, and (5) learning the performance of 3D designs. For the case study, we use the 2D wheel design data (front view) generated by Oh et al. (2019). First, the proposed process maps 2D wheel design data into latent space. Second, 2D wheel design samples are drawn from latent space. Third, 2D wheel design samples are automatically converted to 3D wheel designs. Fourth, 3D wheel designs are automatically evaluated through modal analysis. Finally, 2D designs and model analysis results are used for input and output of deep learning, respectively. In the end, the proposed deep learning model can predict the performance of 3D structural design based on 2D structural design, and we show that the whole evaluation process is done completely automatically.
