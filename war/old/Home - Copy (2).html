Reserch

I am interested in (1) Design for Market Systems to solve enterprise-level design problems by linking engineering, marketing, operations, and public policy models, (2) Design for Perceptual Attributes to understand the impact of product aesthetics on consumers’ preferences by using human-computer interactions, and (3) Systems Design methods that coordinate decomposed subsystems to achieve system-level design objectives.

[1] Design for Market Systems

Product or service attributes are decided by designers from different domains such as engineering, industrial design, operations, and public policy. Therefore, understanding how their decisions are related is necessary when they share design variables that lead to trade-offs of other designers’ goals. My research integrates different design domains into single decision making framework. In particular, I have been working on electric vehicle (EV) and autonomous vehicle design problems by integrating electric powertrain simulation, autonomous fleet assignment simulation, charging station location network, consumer demand, and public policy models, into a large decision making framework. I compared the public investment impacts on EV markets in China and US, and extended this framework to autonomous vehicle sharing services. In addition, I am exploring how to make design decisions today by taking future long-term design evolution into account. I adopt real option theory used in Financial Engineering to hedge design risks and create smooth design transitions.

[2] Design for Perceptual Attributes

Although consumer preference model is a core part in Design for Market Systems, capturing preferences on perceptual attributes is still a challenge. I have been examining the trade-off between forms and functions in a vehicle and its seats. I elicited consumers’ preferences on product design using parameterized 3D shapes and tested the effects of specific design variables on the perception of forms.

[3] Systems Design

One practical issue of Design for Market Systems is that a system problem may not be solvable due to the size and complexity of models. Analytical Target Cascading (ATC) method has been developed to address this issue by partitioning a system into subsystems and coordinating them to meet a system goal. I have been designing commercial vehicle systems and hybrid vehicle architectures using ATC. However, I realized that ATC with parallel solving approach often fails to achieve convergence or involves high computational cost. Therefore, I research on robust convergence strategies for complex industrial applications that should be solved in parallel.

Design of artifacts is addressed from a multidisciplinary perspective that includes engineering, art, psychology, marketing, economics, and other disciplines. 2013 - 2015
Interaction and coordination of decisions based on multi-discipline design analyses is studied in the context of a newly developed artifact. Students propose and test novel analysis methods and design process models.
